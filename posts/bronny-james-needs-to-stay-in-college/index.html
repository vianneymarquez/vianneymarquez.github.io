Bronny James should stay another season playing college basketball.

Author

Vianney Marquez

Published

May 5, 2024

Bronny James, son of All-Star Los Angeles Lakers forward LeBron James, declared for the NBA draft while also entering the NCAA transfer portal. James has the build of a great athlete and is selfless offensively and defensively on the court that gives him NBA potential. But his first college season at USC was underwhelming. Before his first game, he suffered cardiac arrest and was hospitalized for three days. The fact he even was able to play this year was a miracle and shows how tough he is mentally. On top of that, his squad had a losing record and missed the NCAA Tournament entirely with James being statistically low too.

“He should go back to school to develop at his own pace, or he risks getting lost in the shuffle, whether or not he’s playing with his dad,” said an NBA general manager.

Now that we’re heading into summer, the big questions remain should James keep his name in the 2024 NBA draft, or should he stay in college another year?

Let’s take a look at some numbers.

The first question we’ll ask and answer by rating, against all the freshman players who entered the draft, where is James ranked by points?

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(gt)
library(ggbeeswarm)
library(ggrepel)

players24 <- read_csv("players24.csv")

freshman <- c("Bronny James", "Reed Sheppard", "Stephon Castle", "Isaiah Collier", "Jared McCain", "Rob Dillingham", "Cody Williams", "Ja'Kobe Walter", "Johnny Furphy", "D.J. Wagner", "Bobi Klintman","Justin Edwards")

freshman24 <- players24 |> 
  filter(Player %in% freshman)

bronny <- freshman24 |> 
  filter(Player == "Bronny James")

ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=freshman24, aes(x=reorder(Player, PTS), weight=PTS))  +
  geom_bar(data=bronny, aes(x=reorder(Player, PTS), weight=PTS), fill="#990000") +       coord_flip() +
  labs(
    x="Players", 
    y="Points",
    title="Bronny is averaging the lowest points", 
    subtitle="The former USC player best bet is to stay another year in college. ", 
    caption="Source: Sports Reference | By Vianney Marquez"
    ) + 
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 12, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 10), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

With just six starts across 25 games, James averaged just 4.8 points on a bad Trojans team. This put him at the bottom of the totem pole against all the other freshman who have also entered the NBA draft. He’s not even that close to reaching their averages either. Based on just his points, his best bet is to stay another year to increase his shooting average.

Points aren’t the only thing scouts look for when choosing the right player for their team. They want someone who will positively impact their team in both offensive and defensive. So where is James again against the other top-ranked freshman?

Code 
playerswhoplayed <- players24 |> 
  filter(G > 10 & MP > 500) 

bronny <- players24 |> 
  filter(Player == "Bronny James")

ggplot() +

Defensive Box Plus/Minus measures a player’s impact on a team’s defense. It is per-100-possession stat. James’s DBPM is at a 1.4, which is pretty good. He is a defense-first player. He can really disrupt an opposing team’s offense. This is very important to be in the NBA. On the other hand, his OBPM isn’t as good. It is at a -2.6. It seems like he was way too easy to defend and didn’t always positively impact his team offensively.

Since James doesn’t always positively impact him team offensively, what does this say about his usage percentage and how does it compare to some of the highest ranked players right now?

Code 
playerswhoplayed <- players24 |> 
  filter(G > 10 & MP > 500) 

bronny <- players24 |> 
  filter(Player == "Bronny James")

ggplot() +
  geom_beeswarm(
    data=playerswhoplayed, 
    groupOnX=FALSE, 
    aes(x=`USG%`, y=Class), color="grey") + 
  geom_vline(xintercept = 19.4) + 
  geom_beeswarm(
    data=bronny, 
    groupOnX=TRUE, 
    aes(x=`USG%`, y=Class), color="#990000") +
  geom_text(
    aes(x=24.5, y="FR", label="Bronny James")
  ) +
labs(x="USG%", y="Class", title="Bronny's USG% is at a 19.4", subtitle="LeBron James's son usage percentage isn't bad, but a little too low.", caption="Source: Sports Reference | By Vianney Marquez") + 
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 15, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 9), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=11), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

Usage rate calculates what percentage of a team’s offensive possessions a player uses while on the court provided that the play ends in of one of the three results: field-goal attempt, free-throw attempt, or turnover. If you think about it, an average player USG% should be at a 20 percent. James’s USG% is at a 19.4. He is close and right in the middle of his class. This means he preferably likes to pass more than shoot. He is a selfless player or is it because a lot of his shots don’t go in, so he must resort to this?

Suffering a heart issue that set back the start of his season while being on a losing team, would put James in a better position as a sophomore if he chooses to stay in college. There’s a lot to like about James and the way he plays. He could be a 3-and-D combo guard since his stats prove he is not selfish on the court. He could thrive on the right college team that could set him up for a better chance to be selected in the earlier rounds of the draft. As of right now, he should probably stay another season if he wants to see time on the NBA floor.
